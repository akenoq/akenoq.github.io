About the Medtner Society of America

History

The idea of establishing Medtner Society of Korea (MSK) was first proposed in Jan 2016. Two Korean pianists, Dr. Haeshin Shin and Dr. Hanna Choi, having studied together at Seoul National University under professor Hyoung Joon Chang, a pupil of Earl Wild who championed Medtner’s piano music, met in the International Medtner Festival in London and enthusiastically discussed the necessities of launching first Korean society exclusively dedicated to Nikolai Medtner.
Nikolai Medtner seemed to be a rare and unfamiliar composer, especially in Korea. In spite of a remarkable growth and development that dedicated Korean musicians have achieved so far, we find it very difficult to observe Medner’s music in the concert repertoire. We think this is a big loss to musical circles of Korea considering his considerable contribution to piano and vocal genre. Our thoughts have become more solid as we begun our study of his music. Why his extensive artistic endeavors in musical composition have not received the same level of critical attention as his contemporaries, such as Alexander Scriabin and Sergei Rachmaninoff?
This enquiry has motivated us to explore some ways of spotlighting Medtner as a pianist-composer whose musical composition is informed by his pianism, and conveying that Medtner’s musical works, reflecting his virtuoso pianism, can be better understood at the piano, through performing.
Building on these efforts, we hope Medtner Society of Korea 1) could help audiences to have more opportunities of appreciating Medtner’s musical works, 2) could organize various performances, seminars, projects, and collaborations for the purpose of animating his pianistic texture, and 3) could preserve invaluable resources of Medtner as well as communicate the value of his musical world.

About the Founders

Sofiya Schug & Alexei Ulitin

Sofiya Schug

Pianist Haeshin Shin has appeared in recitals, concerts, and festivals throughout the United States, Russia, Scotland, Luxemburg, Belgium, Puerto Rico, China, and Korea. She had the privilege of performing in master classes of John Perry, John O'Conor, Philippe Entremont, Martino Tirimo, Willem Brons, Ian Hobson, Boris Slutsky, Dmitry Rachmanov, Choong Mo Kang, Dai Uk Lee, Yong Hi Moon, and Marilyn Nonken.

Ms. Shin won 1st prize at American Protégé Concerto Competition, 1st prize at American Protégé Romantic Music Competition, 2nd prize at Richmond Piano Competition, and received an honorable mention at Int’l Competition for Pianists in Memory of Shande Ding and Bradshaw and Buono Int’l Competition.

Alexei Ulitin, piano

Alexei Ulitin (Kiev, Ukraine) has recently worked as a musical staff in Siena Music Festival. Ulitin also has attended Aspen Music Festival as a collaborative artist in 2012 and 2011. In addition to performing concerts in Finland, France, Russia, Ukraine, and the United States, Ulitin has won prizes in numerous international competitions, including Liszt-Garrison Festival & International Piano Competition in Baltimore 2013 (2nd prize), the International Young Artist Piano Competition in Washington DC 2010 (1st prize); MTNA competition 2010 (eastern division winner); International Piano and Piano duets Competition in Finland, Juvaskula 2004 (1st prize).

Alexei holds a Bachelor of Music degree from Rowan University (New Jersey) where he studied with Veda Zuponcic. His Master’s degree is from the National Music Academy of Ukriane. Currently, Alexei is a DMA student in Collaborative Piano at University of Maryland, his teacher is Professor Rita Sloan.
